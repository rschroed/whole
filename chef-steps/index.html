OK, I promised a few free ideas, so here they are. I expect you'll get at least what you paid for them, but hopefully they'll give you a glimpse into my thought process.

Part of my inspiration for putting this application together came from finally getting to cook with Joule. Mine finally shipped just before thanksgiving and I got to use it a bunch in the first week I had it.

While using it I found myself opening the app a bunch just to check the temp and timer. It’s not a huge deal of course, but it’s not “glanceable.” One of the things I liked about my Anovoa has the large LCD display I could glance at as I walked by. Two ideas immediately sprang to mind as a way to improve this user experience.

Free Idea #1

JOULE WIDGET

This first one is a total gimmie, in fact I assume it’s already designed and just waiting for QA and/or app store approval. An iOS widget would be a great way to quickly check the status of a cook without having to go in proper Joule app.

Free Idea #2

JOULE REMOTE

The Joule app is amazing, but hypothetically, let’s say you’re busy making dinner and your kid won’t shut up about the latest Pokemon he found and his brother is threatening to dump flour all over the kitchen so you just hand them your phone in the hopes that YouTube Kids shuts them up long enough for you to finish the pork chops and make a salad, how do you check the time and temp then?

The Joule Remote, that’s how.

Check the status of your current cook, or set the time and temp for a new one. With it’s E-Ink touchscreen display it’s easy to use and the battery lasts practically forever. When the battery does get low, just flip out the plug and stick it in an outlet.
